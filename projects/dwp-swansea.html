The brief.

The Department for Work & Pensions required a full electrical fit-out of their new Swansea offices. Because it's a government site with public-facing operations, life-safety systems — addressable fire detection, disabled refuge alarm and emergency lighting — had to be installed and commissioned alongside the main electrical package, with zero slippage to the programme.

What we delivered.

A single, coordinated electrical package. Our team handled containment, switchgear, power distribution, DALI lighting, the addressable fire alarm system and the disabled refuge alarm — with everything tied back to a BS 7671 certification pack and a BAFE SP203-1 fire commissioning document.

  • All containment — basket, tray and trunking — on a coordinated route designed around the mechanical services.
  • Switchgear install — distribution boards, sub-mains and final circuit termination.
  • Power distribution to the workstations, server room, comms rooms and welfare facilities.
  • DALI lighting throughout, commissioned to daylight and occupancy profiles for energy reporting.
  • Addressable fire alarm to BS 5839-1 with zone-plan drawings and commissioning certificate.
  • Disabled refuge alarm on each escape route, interfaced with the fire panel.

Why it mattered.

Public-sector programmes are unforgiving of slippage — a late electrical finish holds up the occupation date, which in turn holds up the operational service behind it. Delivering on schedule and on budget, with no defects at hand-over, is the reason DWP projects keep coming back.
