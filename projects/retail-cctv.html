The brief.

A major high-street retailer needed a single accredited partner to take on integrated CCTV across their UK estate and their continental European expansion — new stores, refits and replacements for ageing analogue systems. Downtime on a trading store is measured in lost revenue per minute, so every site had to be mobilised without disrupting trade.

What we delivered.

We designed the system to a common specification, then flexed the install per site. Cameras, recorders, cabling and head-end gear are standardised so that spares, configuration and operator training transfer cleanly from one store to the next. Every install is commissioned with a full BS EN 62676 document pack handed to the retailer's loss-prevention team.

  • Per-site design based on store layout, till counts, stockroom access and high-value product zones.
  • Supply of cameras, NVRs, switches, cabling and monitors to a standardised BoM.
  • Out-of-hours installation to avoid trading disruption — nights and early mornings scheduled across regional sweeps.
  • Commissioning with image-quality, frame-rate and coverage verification against the BS EN 62676 design brief.
  • Handover pack — as-built drawings, commissioning certificates, operator manuals and maintenance schedule.
  • 24/7 support with remote health monitoring and an engineer response SLA.

Why it mattered.

A retailer with a European footprint needs a contractor who can mobilise teams across borders and still deliver a paper trail that satisfies UK insurers, EU data-protection regulators and their own loss-prevention team. Our SSAIB certification — UKAS-accredited and recognised by police and insurers — made the procurement decision straightforward.
