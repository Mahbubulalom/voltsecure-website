What each certification means.

Understand what each certification covers and why it matters for your project.

BAFEFire Compliance
Third-party certified for fire protection services.
+

What it is

BAFE (British Approvals for Fire Equipment) is the independent register of quality fire safety service providers in the UK. BAFE-registered companies are assessed by UKAS-accredited certification bodies.

What this means for clients

Guarantees that your fire alarm and detection systems are designed, installed and maintained by a company meeting the highest fire-safety standards. Essential for insurance compliance and regulatory sign-off.

Services covered

Fire alarm design & installFire detection systemsEmergency lighting
NICEICElectrical
Approved contractor for electrical installation and testing.
+

What it is

NICEIC (National Inspection Council for Electrical Installation Contracting) is the UK's leading voluntary regulatory body for the electrical contracting industry. Approved Contractors are assessed against the current edition of BS 7671.

What this means for clients

Your electrical installations are carried out by qualified engineers who are regularly reassessed. All work is covered by NICEIC's complaints process and insurance-backed guarantee.

Services covered

Commercial electrical installTesting & inspectionElectrical maintenance
SSAIBSecurity
Approved for design, installation and maintenance of electronic security systems.
+

What it is

SSAIB is a leading certification body for organisations providing security systems, fire detection and alarm systems, and telecare services. SSAIB is a UKAS-accredited certification body.

What this means for clients

Assurance that security and fire systems are designed, installed, commissioned and maintained to the relevant European and British Standards. Required by many insurers and local authorities.

Services covered

Intruder alarmsAccess controlCCTV systems
ECAElectrical Trade
Electrical Contractors' Association membership.
+

What it is

The ECA (Electrical Contractors' Association) is the UK's leading trade association for electrical, electrotechnical and energy services companies. Membership demonstrates technical competence and financial stability.

What this means for clients

ECA members are regularly assessed and must demonstrate ongoing competence. ECA membership also provides access to dispute resolution and an insurance-backed warranty scheme.

Services covered

Electrical contractingEnergy servicesElectrotechnical work

NSI approval means our security and fire installations meet the standards required by police, fire services, insurers and local authorities.
