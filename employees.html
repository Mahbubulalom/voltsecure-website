Welcome to VoltSecure.

Whether you're a first-week apprentice or a senior engineer, this page brings together the things you need most often - certifications, benefits, training and who to ask when something comes up.

Pay, leave & benefits

  • JIB-graded pay scale. Every role mapped to the Joint Industry Board grade for fair, transparent rates.
  • Pension. Employer contribution from day one - rate confirmed in your offer letter.
  • Holiday. Statutory minimum plus extra service-based days - see your contract.
  • Sick pay. Company sick-pay scheme above SSP, subject to length of service.
  • Tools & PPE. Company-issued PPE, branded workwear and full personal tool kit. Replacements through your project manager.
  • Vehicle. Marked company vans for engineers; EV transition in progress where charging makes operational sense.

Training & certifications

VoltSecure is registered for funded apprenticeships in electrical installation and FESS (Fire, Emergency & Security Systems). All engineers hold or are working toward the certifications below:

  • ECS Gold or relevant grade card
  • BS 7671 (18th Edition) qualification, where applicable
  • BAFE SP203-1 modules for fire-system roles
  • FIA / Skills for Security CPD on rolling cycle
  • Manufacturer training (HikVision, Paxton, Texecom, Advanced, etc.) - booked through your project manager

Renewal reminders go out automatically 90 days before any certification expires. Don't wait for the email - flag it to the office as soon as you know.

What we expect, day to day

  • Compliance is the standard, not the ceiling. Every job delivered to the relevant British / European Standard, every certificate signed off properly.
  • Tidy site, tidy paperwork. Sites are left as we found them or better; documentation is uploaded the same day, not at month-end.
  • Speak up. If something on site looks wrong, off-spec, or unsafe - stop work and call your PM. We'd rather lose an hour than create a defect.
  • Look after each other. Apprentices learn from engineers, engineers learn from each other. Mentoring is part of the job, not a side task.
